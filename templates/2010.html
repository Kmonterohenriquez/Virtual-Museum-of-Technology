2011 – Nest Learning Thermostat released
Nest Learning Thermostat:

Nest Labs introduced the first smart thermostat in 2011. The first Nest thermostat is able to learn from the user’s behavior and its surroundings to create a custom heating and cooling schedule. Its main features are that it programs itself, saves energy, and can connect to Wi-Fi so the temperature can be controlled from a computer, smartphone, or tablet.

2012 – Raspberry Pi
Raspberry Pi:

The Raspberry Pi is a small (credit card-sized), low-cost computer that first debuted in 2012. It’s used by being plugged into a computer monitor or TV and functions with a keyboard and mouse. Raspberry Pi has become popular for being a welcoming way for people to explore computing and learn programming by enabling them to engage in their own computing projects.

2015 – Apple Watch released
Apple Watch:

The Apple Watch, first released in 2015, gave people a way to basically keep their laptop or iPhone on their wrist at all times. The Apple Watch connects to an iPhone and as long as the phone is connected to Wi-Fi or cellular data, it can access applications and send and receive emails and texts.

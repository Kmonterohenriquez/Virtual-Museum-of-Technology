1996 – PalmPilot – Palm1000
Palm Pilot:

The Palm 1000 was a notable handheld device called a PDA, or Personal Digital Assistant. It was first released in 1996 and kind of acted as a modern day smartphone. It could be used to make and maintain a schedule, included applications, and could connect to a PC or Mac computer. While the Palm company is not relevant anymore, their PDA’s were a first of its kind sparking further innovation for the more advanced smartphone.

1998 – First portable MP3 player – MPMan Music Player
First portable MP3 player:

In 1998, the world’s first portable MP3 player was released by Saehan Information Systems called the MPMan with its main, and only, function being to store and play music. It had a small display screen and only 32MB of memory, so by the time future MP3 players came out, the MPMan saw less use. However, it did introduce the idea of portable music from which many other companies drew inspiration from.

1981 – Osborne 1 – First mass-produced portable microcomputer – Lee Felsenstein
Osborne 1:

The Osborne 1 was the first mass-produced portable computer which was released in 1981. It weighed 24 pounds, costed $1,795 and featured a 5-inch display, 64 KB of memory, a modem, and two 5.25-inch floppy disk drives. The creation of the Osborne 1 helped lead the way for the production of laptops which are considerably less expensive, lighter, and more functional.

1981 – IBM Personal Computer released – Model 5150
IBM Personal Computer:

In 1981, IBM introduced its first personal computer, the IBM Model 5150. It used Microsoft’s MS-DOS operating system and was based on a 4.77 MHz Intel 8088 microprocessor.

1989 – Macintosh Portable introduced
Macintosh Portable:

Apple released their first truly portable computer, in 1989. While the first Mac “laptop” was heavy (sixteen pounds), expensive ($6,500 US dollars), and had bad sales, this computer was the start of numerous innovations which would involve into the Apple laptops we have now.

1971 – First microprocessor – Intel and Ted Hoff Intel 4004
Microprocessor:

Intel introduced the first microprocessor, the 4004, in 1971 with the help of Ted Hoff who led the architecture creation. The microprocessor acts as the brain of computers and many other electronic devices. It controls the functions of the CPU, or central processing unit, of a computer, and gives and receives instructions and preforms tasks involved in computer processing, such as calculations that make the computer function.

1973 – First mobile phone call made
First Mobile Phone Call Made:

On April 3, 1973, the first mobile phone call was made by Martin Cooper. Cooper was a Motorola employee and was using a prototype of what would become world’s first commercial cell phone, the Motorola Dyna TAC 8000x. The call was made from Manhattan and placed to the headquarters of Bell Labs in New Jersey with the first words being something to the effect of, “I’m ring you just to see if my call sounds good at your end.” Apple 1: The first Apple computer, the Apple-1, was completed by Steve Wozniak in 1976 and marketed by his friend, Steve Jobs. It was a single-board computer which sold about 200 units before Apple announced the Apple II, which sold millions of units for about two decades. While the first Apple computers were a humble start to the company, Apple has come a long way in terms of devices and technological advancements.

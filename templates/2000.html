2000 – First USB flash drive sold in US (invented 1999)
First USB Flash Drive sold in US:

While the first USB flash drive was created in 1999, it was not officially sold until 2000. USB flash drives, also known as jump drives, can store various types of files and data backups along with applications and are small enough to fit on a keychain. The first flash drive had a storage capacity of 8 megabytes, and for comparison, most flash drives now have at least 8 gigabytes of storage. The first USB flash drive made it possible for computer users to store and take important files and work etc. on the go with them

2001 – Civilian use of GPS machines
GPS Machines:

GPS machine technology was originally just for military use, but as technology started advancing and GPS receiver technology got smaller and less expensive to produce, companies started to then use if for in-car navigation. This has expanded into having location services on computers and smartphones that have AI, or artificial intelligence, incorporated in them.

2001 – First Apple iPod released
Apple iPod:

In 2001, Apple released its first version of an MP3 player, called the iPod. While it didn’t have a touch screen or Bluetooth, it did have a simply design that was easy for people to use, which caused it to become the front-runner of MP3 players at the time.

2007 – iPhone released
iPhone:

in 2007, Steve Jobs revealed to the public what Apple had been working on for nearly 3 years, the iPhone. It was introduced as a mobile phone with a touch screen that could communicate with the internet. The iPhone was not the first device labeled as a smartphone to be invented, but it did leave a mark and set the standard for future smartphones.
